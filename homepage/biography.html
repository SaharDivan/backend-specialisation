Biography

Sahar Sahebdivan(i) (*1982, Shiraz) is a Persian Iranian physicist. She received her PhD in theoretical physics from University of St Andrews, UK, under supervision of Prof. Ulf Leonhardt. Her interest in physics, in a broad term, is in the foundation of quantum mechanics and its interplay with gravity. Currently, She is a researcher at the University of Vienna, devision of quantum optics, quantum information and nanophysics, in the group of Prof. Aspelmeyer where the group is pioneer​ in quantum optomechanics experiments, precise quantum measurements and testing the foundation of quantum mechanics. At the final stage of her PhD she was involved in the QUEST project, a flagship project of the Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council (UK),for two years. Until her postgraduate studies (MSc degree) she lived in Iran. Her BSc and MSc studies was focused on theoretical and mathematical physics including the causal structure of space-time, foundation of quantum mechanics and quantization theories. She has written her thesis on the transition process from quantum to classics in the framework of Berezin quantization theory. Her PhD research (thesis submission: December 2014) dealed with classical fields on curved manifolds, differential geometry and the mathematical structure of general relativity (curved space-times'). Particularly, the focus was on controlling classical electrodynamics fields through non-Euclidian geometries -- inside inhomogeneous media--and its applications in optical science​.​ An example of such an optical manipulation is diffraction-free imaging. Her PhD thesis was devoted to designing and analyse of an optical instrument utilising geometry to reach the ultimate resolution beyond the diffraction limit.
